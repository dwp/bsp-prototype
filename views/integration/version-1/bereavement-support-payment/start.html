Bereavement Support Payment

A husband, wife or civil partner can apply for Bereavement Support Payment (BSP) if they were under State Pension age when their partner died.

What you can get

BSP is a lump sum payment and then up to 18 monthly payments.

Lump sum Monthly payments
Standard rate £2,500 £100
Higher rate £3,500 £350

If you get Child Benefit (or do not get it but are entitled to it), you will get the higher rate.

If you do not get Child Benefit, you will get the standard rate unless you were pregnant when your husband, wife or civil partner died.

You must claim within 3 months of the death to get the full amount. You can claim up to 21 months after their death but you will get fewer monthly payments.

Eligibility

When they died, you must have been:

  • married or in a civil partnership with them
  • under State Pension age
  • living in the UK or a country that pays bereavement benefit

Your partner must have either:

  • paid National Insurance contributions for at least 25 weeks in a year
  • died because of an accident at work or a disease caused by work

BSP is not a means-tested benefit.
