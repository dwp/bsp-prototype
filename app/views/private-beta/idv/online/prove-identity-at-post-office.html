Prove your identity at a Post Office

You can prove your identity at a Post Office instead.

You’ll be asked to:

  • enter details from your photo ID on GOV.UK
  • go to a Post Office where they’ll scan your ID and take a photo of you

You’ll usually get a result within a day of going to the Post Office.

  • Types of photo ID you can use at a Post Office

    You’ll need one of the following:

    • a UK photocard driving licence (full or provisional)
    • a UK passport
    • a non-UK passport
    • a UK biometric residence permit (BRP)
    • a European Union (EU) photocard driving licence
    • a national identity card from a European Economic Area (EEA) country
You cannot prove your identity at a Post Office with a Frontier Worker permit (FWP) or a UK biometric residence card (BRC), also called a UK residence card.
